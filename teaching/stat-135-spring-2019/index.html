Stat 135 (Spring 2019)

I served as a GSI for this upper division undergraduate course providing a first introduction to mathematical statistics. Based on problems provided by the Instructor Adam Lucas, I prepared and used slides to facilitate team work during section. I also occasionally wrote notes for students to supplement my in-class teaching. Here is a thematic summary I typed up for students to strategize for their final exam review.

  • Week 1: Introduction
  • Week 2: Sampling, parametric models / R setup and practice
  • Week 3: MoM estimation / bootstrap practice [slides]
  • Week 4: Maximum likelihood estimation, CRLB [slides]
  • Week 5: Sufficiency [slides]
  • Week 6: Hypothesis testing, Neyman-Pearson lemma, duality of HT and CI [slides] [notes]
  • Week 7: Comparing two samples: parametric
  • Week 8: (Midterm review) [jeopardy slides]
  • Week 9: Likelihood ratio test, UMP test, GLRT [slides]
  • Week 10: (Spring break)
  • Week 11: Comparing two samples: non-parametric, chi-square tests
  • Week 12: ANOVA
  • Week 13: Linear model (single predictor), least squares [notes]
  • Week 14: Linear model (multiple predictors)
  • Week 15: Bayesian statistics [slides]
