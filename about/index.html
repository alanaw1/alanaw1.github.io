About Me

I am a second-year PhD student in the Department of Statistics at the University of California at Berkeley. I am broadly interested in statistical modeling, statistical theory, and their applications to biology and the social sciences.

I completed my undergraduate studies in Mathematical and Computational Science (with a Biology minor) at Stanford University, where I was advised by Bradley Efron. I was fortunate to do research in mathematical and computational biology with Noah Rosenberg, Marcus Feldman and Julia Palacios. My projects included modeling evolution, phylogenetics, population genetics and statistical genetics. I also spent a summer at the University of Michigan working with Jun Zhang on mathematical psychology.

Before coming to the US, I completed military service with the Singapore Armed Forces, and finished junior college and secondary school at Raffles Institution. I worked with Cheng Yeaw Ku on probabilistic combinatorics and coding theory, and spent a short time with the Department of Modulation and Coding at the Institute for Infocomm Research, A*STAR.

I also have experience in STEM communication and writing with Asian Scientist Magazine.
