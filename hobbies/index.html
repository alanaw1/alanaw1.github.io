Hobbies

Science Communication 🎶

Growing up a slow learner in a household that spoke broken English, I struggled with communicating clearly. Experiencing the negative consequences of poor communication (ex: lack of confidence, poor grades in English class) motivated me to improve my communication skills.

Together with my interest in mathematics and the winds of fortune, my educational voyage led me to stumble upon Asian Scientist (now part of Wildtype Media Group). There, I was incredibly fortunate to learn from and be mentored by amazing scientists and writers like Dr. Juliana Chan and Dr. Rebecca Tan. I learned to think critically about how science can be impactful to society in many ways, to recognize the positive outcomes that increased science communication brings about for society, and to encourage science communication in Asia, where it is lacking.

Above all else, I discovered I really do enjoy writing clearly about science!

While I have little time to write these days, I continue to integrate what I have learned into the way I share my scientific research. There’s an inexplicable satisfaction when someone experiences the same “aha!” that you do after understanding a complex idea.
